===========================================================================
  JUSTIFICATION FOR FOUR-LEG, TWO-LANE INTERSECTION GEOMETRY
===========================================================================

This document provides arguments and references to justify the choice
of a 4-leg signalized intersection with 2 through lanes per approach
as the simulation scenario.


===========================================================================
1. REPRESENTATIVENESS OF FOUR-LEG SIGNALIZED INTERSECTIONS
===========================================================================

Four-leg intersections are the most common intersection geometry where
two roads cross. According to the FHWA Signalized Intersections
Informational Guide (FHWA-HRT-04-091):

  "The most common intersection where two highways cross
   has four legs." (Chapter 3)

The United States has approximately 330,000 signalized intersections
(ITE estimate, 2018). FHWA reports that roughly 10% of all ~3 million
U.S. intersections are signalized, and signalized intersections account
for approximately one-third of all intersection-related fatalities.

The four-leg signalized intersection is the standard configuration for
urban arterial crossings — the exact deployment environment targeted
by C-V2X V2V safety applications per SAE J3161 and J2945/1.


===========================================================================
2. SAFETY RELEVANCE: CONFLICT POINT DENSITY
===========================================================================

A key reason to focus on four-leg intersections is their high conflict
point density. Per FHWA-HRT-04-091 (Chapter 3, Section 3.2):

  - Three-leg intersection: 9 conflict points
    (3 diverging + 3 merging + 3 crossing)

  - Four-leg intersection: 32 conflict points
    (8 diverging + 8 merging + 16 crossing)

The jump from 9 to 32 conflict points represents a 3.6x increase in
potential vehicle-vehicle conflicts. The 16 crossing conflicts (where
vehicle paths intersect at near-right-angles) are the most severe
collision type and are the primary target of V2X safety applications
such as Intersection Movement Assist (IMA) and Red Light Violation
Warning (RLVW).

This makes the four-leg intersection a worst-case-representative
scenario for evaluating V2X safety message reliability: if PQC
authentication can maintain adequate PDR and latency at a geometry
with 32 conflict points, it will perform at least as well at simpler
three-leg intersections.


===========================================================================
3. MAXIMUM CONCURRENT TRANSMITTERS AND CHANNEL STRESS
===========================================================================

A four-leg intersection creates a unique stress condition for C-V2X
Mode 4 sidelink: vehicles from ALL FOUR approaches converge within
mutual communication range simultaneously. This produces the highest
density of concurrent sidelink transmitters in a localized area.

Compare with other geometries:
  - Highway: Vehicles are distributed linearly; at any point, only
    vehicles within ±300m compete for resources.
  - T-intersection (3-leg): Only 3 approaches contribute transmitters.
  - Four-leg intersection: 4 approach flows + 4 departure flows +
    turning movements all share the same sidelink resource pool within
    the intersection's communication footprint.

For a study evaluating whether PQC's larger packets can coexist with
Mode 4 resource allocation, the four-leg intersection provides the
most challenging (and therefore most informative) test case.


===========================================================================
4. TWO THROUGH LANES PER APPROACH — JUSTIFICATION
===========================================================================

Two through lanes per approach is the standard urban arterial cross-
section for minor-to-major arterials (FHWA Functional Classification
Guidelines, 2013). This choice is justified by:

(a) REPRESENTATIVENESS: Two-lane arterials (one lane per direction,
    or two lanes per direction at intersections including turn pocket)
    are the most common urban arterial type. Wider cross-sections
    (3+ through lanes) are typically limited to major arterials and
    expressways in large metropolitan areas.

(b) MODERATE COMPLEXITY: One-lane approaches would produce too few
    vehicles to stress the sidelink (unrealistically easy). Three-lane
    or four-lane approaches would primarily increase vehicle counts
    without introducing new Mode 4 dynamics. Two lanes provide enough
    vehicles to generate meaningful sidelink contention while remaining
    representative of typical urban intersections.

(c) HCM CAPACITY ALIGNMENT: The HCM saturation flow rate of
    1,800 pc/h/ln is calibrated for standard urban intersection lanes.
    With 2 through lanes + signal timing, the approach capacity of
    approximately 1,800 veh/h (at g/C ≈ 0.5) aligns with realistic
    peak-hour volumes for minor arterials. This allows the LOS A-F
    range to be explored with realistic flow rates.


===========================================================================
5. PRECEDENT IN V2X SIMULATION LITERATURE
===========================================================================

The four-leg signalized intersection is the de facto standard scenario
in C-V2X and DSRC simulation studies:

  - Gonzalez-Martin et al. (2019) evaluated Mode 4 PDR using a
    Manhattan grid of four-leg intersections.
  - Toghi et al. (2019) used four-leg intersections for congestion
    analysis in Mode 4 networks.
  - McCarthy et al. (2020) — the openCV2X paper — evaluated Mode 4
    performance at intersections.
  - Twardokus & Rahbari (2025) — the CHASM paper on PQC for C-V2X —
    also used intersection scenarios.

Using the same intersection geometry ensures comparability with the
existing literature and allows direct benchmarking against published
Mode 4 PDR results.


===========================================================================
6. NLOS PROPAGATION RELEVANCE
===========================================================================

The four-leg intersection is uniquely suited for evaluating NLOS
channel conditions because vehicles on perpendicular approaches are
in mutual NLOS until they reach the intersection itself. Buildings
at the four corners block cross-street sightlines, creating the
exact propagation scenario modeled by the ITU-R M.2135-1 Urban
Microcell NLOS model.

This geometry naturally creates a mix of:
  - LOS conditions: vehicles on the same approach leg
  - NLOS conditions: vehicles on perpendicular approach legs
  - Transitioning conditions: vehicles entering/exiting the
    intersection box

This mixed LOS/NLOS environment is more representative of real urban
V2X deployment than a pure highway scenario (predominantly LOS) and
allows evaluation of PQC performance under both propagation conditions
within a single scenario.

7. SUGGESTED TEXT FOR THE PAPER
===========================================================================

Below is suggested text that can be added to the Traffic Scenario
subsection (Section IV-C):

--- BEGIN SUGGESTED TEXT ---

The four-leg signalized intersection is selected as the evaluation
scenario for three reasons. First, it is the most common intersection
geometry where two roads cross in the U.S. urban road network, and it
represents the standard deployment environment for C-V2X V2V safety
applications~\cite{sae_j3161}. Second, a four-leg intersection
produces 32 potential vehicle-vehicle conflict points (16 of which are
crossing conflicts), compared to only 9 at a three-leg intersection,
making it the most safety-critical intersection type for V2X
applications such as Intersection Movement Assist
(IMA)~\cite{fhwa_sig_guide}. Third, the four-leg geometry creates a
high concentration of concurrent sidelink transmitters from all four
approaches within mutual communication range, providing the most
demanding test of Mode~4 resource allocation under PQC-induced payload
overhead. The two-lane-per-approach cross-section represents a typical
urban minor arterial, with approach capacity aligned to the HCM
saturation flow rate of 1{,}800~pc/h/ln~\cite{hcm2010}. Additionally,
vehicles on perpendicular approaches experience NLOS propagation
conditions due to corner building obstruction, enabling evaluation of
PQC performance under both LOS and NLOS channel conditions within a
single intersection scenario.

--- END SUGGESTED TEXT ---
