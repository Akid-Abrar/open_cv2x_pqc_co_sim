critical because:
  - CBR drives the SPS resource selection in Mode 4
  - Higher PQC overhead → more subchannels → higher CBR → more collisions
  - This creates a feedback loop that aggregate PER misses

CONTRIBUTION: "Analysis of PQC-induced channel congestion via CBR metrics,
revealing the feedback loop between packet size and resource contention."

WHAT CHASM DOES THAT YOU DON'T (acknowledge these)
================================================================================

You should acknowledge these as CHASM's strengths / your limitations:

A. HYBRID (dual) signatures — CHASM signs each BSM with BOTH ECDSA + PQC.
   Your approach is PQC-only replacement (cleaner comparison but no backward
   compatibility during transition period).

B. Certificate fragmentation — CHASM fragments large certs across multiple
   SPDUs. Your approach sends full cert every time (worst-case but simpler).

C. Formal security proofs — CHASM provides EUF-CMA proofs. Your work is
   empirical/simulation-based.

D. Hardware validation — CHASM uses USRP SDRs for over-the-air experiments.
   Your work is simulation-only.

E. Adaptive MCS selection — CHASM's context-aware MCS uses neighbor locations
   to estimate SNR. Your simulation uses standard MCS selection per J3161.
