Title: Impact of Post-Quantum Cryptographic Signatures on LTE-V2X Sidelink
       Performance for Connected Vehicle Safety Messaging

Abstract:

Connected vehicle safety applications rely on digitally signed Basic Safety
Messages (BSMs) broadcast over LTE-V2X (C-V2X) Mode 4 sidelink. Current
deployments use ECDSA P-256 signatures per IEEE 1609.2, but the anticipated
arrival of quantum computing threatens this cryptographic foundation. Post-
quantum cryptography (PQC) algorithms such as Falcon-512 and Dilithium-2
offer quantum-resistant alternatives, yet their significantly larger
signatures and public keys — up to 12x the size of ECDSA — raise concerns
about radio channel capacity. This study evaluates the impact of PQC
algorithms on V2X sidelink performance using a standards-compliant
simulation built on SimuLTE and SUMO, incorporating IEEE 1609.2 security
structures, SAE J2735 BSM encoding, and SAE J3161 radio parameters. Key
performance indicators including packet delivery ratio, end-to-end latency,
channel busy ratio, and resource utilization are compared across algorithms
under varying vehicle densities to assess the feasibility of PQC deployment
in safety-critical transportation systems.
