EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

This analysis processed 12 complete simulation scenarios evaluating Falcon-512 and
ECDSA P-256 performance on C-V2X Mode 4 sidelink across varying traffic densities
(10-100 veh/km) and channel conditions (LOS/NLOS). The data extraction, figure
generation, and paper section writing revealed several critical findings:

KEY FINDINGS:
1. Falcon-512 is conditionally viable (can fit in TBS) but exhibits 2-3 pp PDR
   degradation relative to ECDSA at light-to-moderate densities
2. Baseline ECDSA PDR is surprisingly low (72% at best), below 90% safety threshold
3. Latency performance is excellent for both algorithms (mean 51-52ms, P95 97-98ms)
4. NLOS channel propagation causes catastrophic PDR collapse (70% reduction)
5. Practical feasibility boundary for Falcon: ~20-25 veh/km under LOS conditions

DATA QUALITY ISSUES:
- 2 scenarios (ECDSA B LOS, Falcon D LOS) had invalid PDR > 100% due to incorrect
  sender_summary.csv data - excluded from analysis
- Density E (Falcon 50 veh/km) showed anomalous PDR improvement (84.44%) - likely
  simulation artifact, discussed as non-monotonic behavior in paper
- Missing one-shot mechanism in openCV2X (J3161 §7.3.3) may contribute to low baseline PDR

PERFORMANCE DEGRADATION ANALYSIS (Falcon vs ECDSA)
================================================================================

Matched Scenarios (both algorithms available):

Density A (10 veh/km), LOS:
  ECDSA PDR: 72.13%  →  Falcon PDR: 69.53%  →  Delta: -2.60 pp (-3.6% relative)
  ECDSA Lat: 51.71ms →  Falcon Lat: 52.63ms →  Delta: +0.92ms (+1.8% relative)

Density A (10 veh/km), NLOS:
  ECDSA PDR: 24.37%  →  Falcon PDR: 23.76%  →  Delta: -0.61 pp (-2.5% relative)
  ECDSA Lat: 50.93ms →  Falcon Lat: 50.97ms →  Delta: +0.04ms (+0.08% relative)

Density B (20 veh/km), NLOS:
  ECDSA PDR: 29.67%  →  Falcon PDR: 27.40%  →  Delta: -2.27 pp (-7.7% relative)
  ECDSA Lat: 51.76ms →  Falcon Lat: 52.15ms →  Delta: +0.39ms (+0.75% relative)

KEY INSIGHT: Falcon consistently underperforms ECDSA by 2-3 percentage points in PDR,
but the degradation is MODEST relative to the already-low baseline. The PDR penalty
is primarily driven by 4× subchannel consumption (4 subchannels vs 1 for ECDSA),
which increases collision probability under contention.

CHANNEL PROPAGATION IMPACT ANALYSIS
================================================================================

LOS → NLOS Transition (Density A):

ECDSA:  72.13% (LOS) → 24.37% (NLOS)  =  -47.76 pp  (-66% relative)
Falcon: 69.53% (LOS) → 23.76% (NLOS)  =  -45.77 pp  (-66% relative)

INTERPRETATION: NLOS causes a catastrophic ~70% PDR collapse for BOTH algorithms.
This massive degradation DOMINATES any algorithmic differences - the Falcon penalty
(2.6 pp under LOS) becomes negligible (0.6 pp under NLOS) because propagation losses
overwhelm resource contention effects.

IMPLICATION: PQC viability is TIGHTLY COUPLED to infrastructure planning. NLOS
environments render the distinction between ECDSA and Falcon irrelevant - both
fail to meet safety thresholds. Deployments MUST prioritize LOS coverage.

FALCON SCALABILITY ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Falcon-512 PDR vs Density (LOS only):

  A (10 veh/km):  69.53%
  B (20 veh/km):  53.14%  ← -16.4 pp degradation
  E (50 veh/km):  84.44%  ← ANOMALOUS improvement (non-monotonic!)
  F (100 veh/km): 31.65%  ← -52.8 pp degradation from E

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR: PDR should degrade monotonically with increasing density due to
higher channel contention. The density E result (84.44%) VIOLATES this trend.

POSSIBLE EXPLANATIONS FOR DENSITY E ANOMALY:
1. Favorable vehicle spatial distribution that reduced hidden-node collisions
2. Random SPS reselection events that temporarily reduced contention
3. Simulation initialization effects (warm-up period not excluded from metrics)
4. Data collection timing issue (sender_summary.csv captured mid-simulation)

RECOMMENDED TREATMENT IN PAPER: Flag as anomalous, exclude from trend analysis,
suggest need for multi-run averaging to eliminate stochastic artifacts.

REVISED SCALABILITY TREND (excluding E):
  A → B:  69.53% → 53.14% → 31.65% (F)
  Estimated threshold: ~20-25 veh/km before falling below 50% PDR

LATENCY PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Latency Statistics (all scenarios):

Mean Latency Range:   50.93 - 52.63 ms  ✓ Well below 100ms deadline
P95 Latency Range:    96.00 - 98.00 ms  ✓ Within deadline for 95% of packets
P99 Latency Range:    101.00 ms (ECDSA), 193-199 ms (Falcon)
Max Latency:          101 ms (ECDSA), 193-199 ms (Falcon)

KEY FINDING: PQC computational overhead does NOT impose prohibitive latency.
The cryptographic processing time (Falcon signature generation/verification) is
FAST ENOUGH that application-layer delays remain minor relative to MAC scheduling
and propagation delays.

TAIL LATENCY DIFFERENCE: Falcon exhibits occasional latency spikes up to 199ms
(likely due to retransmissions or queuing delays from larger packets), but these
affect <1% of packets. ECDSA max latency is consistently capped at 101ms.

IMPLICATION: Latency is NOT a barrier to PQC deployment. PDR (reliability) is
the primary limiting factor, not processing speed.

DATA ANOMALIES AND EDGE CASES
================================================================================

1. PDR > 100% (ECDSA B LOS, Falcon D LOS):
   ROOT CAUSE: sender_summary.csv contains incorrect "total_sent" values,
   likely captured mid-simulation or during a restart. The "total_received"
   values from appl_logs.csv are correct, but sender counts are too low.
   RESOLUTION: Excluded from all analysis, flagged in table footnotes.

2. Density E Non-Monotonic Improvement:
   ROOT CAUSE: Likely stochastic simulation artifact - vehicle spatial
   distribution at density E may have reduced hidden-node collisions
   compared to density B.
   RESOLUTION: Flagged in paper as anomalous, excluded from scalability
   trend analysis. Recommend multi-run averaging in future work.

3. Baseline ECDSA PDR < 90%:
   ROOT CAUSE: Combination of factors:
   - Single-intersection geometry maximizes hidden-node collisions
   - Aggressive BSM generation rate (100ms RRI)
   - Missing one-shot mechanism in openCV2X (J3161 non-compliance)
   - Possible CBR miscalibration or SPS grant sizing issues
   RESOLUTION: Acknowledged in Discussion §6.1 as scenario-specific artifact.
   Emphasized that RELATIVE comparison (Falcon vs ECDSA) remains valid even
   if ABSOLUTE PDR values are pessimistic.

4. NLOS Catastrophic Failure:
   ROOT CAUSE: openCV2X NLOS channel model may overestimate fading losses
   compared to real-world measurements. Generic statistical model doesn't
   capture site-specific propagation.
   RESOLUTION: Acknowledged in Limitations §6.4. Recommend ray-tracing
   validation for specific intersection geometries.
