CRITICAL REVIEW: IEEE CNS 2026 SUBMISSION
"Comparative Performance Evaluation of Classical and Post-Quantum
Digital Signatures on C-V2X Sidelink Communications"

--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 1: REDUNDANCIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------

R1. Spectrum-vs-computational bottleneck conclusion is stated four times
    - Abstract (last two sentences)
    - Section III-A, last paragraph (latency analysis)
    - Section III-C, last paragraph ("Taken together, the PDR and
      latency results establish...")
    - Conclusion, first paragraph
    One clear statement in the conclusion and a brief reference
    in the abstract is sufficient. The two middle occurrences can be
    condensed or removed.

R2. SPS mechanism is described twice
    - Method section, lines beginning "In Mode 4, vehicles autonomously
      select transmission resources using sensing-based SPS..."
    - Section III-A (Spectrum Resource Utilization), opening sentences
      repeat the same sensing-window and threshold logic.
    Section III-A should assume the reader has absorbed the Method
    description and skip straight to its implication for Falcon-512.

R3. Four-subchannel vs one-subchannel fact is stated four times
    - Background paragraph 2 ("requires multiple subchannels")
    - Method, size-inflation paragraph ("four subchannels for its
      1,739-byte SPDU")
    - Method, TB-size paragraph ("compared with the 305-byte ECDSA SPDU")
    - Section III opening paragraph and Section III-A opening paragraph
    State it once with the number in the Method section and use a
    cross-reference ("the fourfold overhead established in Section II")
    elsewhere instead of restating the value.

R4. Packet sizes (1,739 B / 305 B / 745 B / 143 B) are repeated in
    the Method, the Results opening paragraph, and Table I. The Results
    paragraph before Figure 2 largely duplicates what Figure 2 and
    Table I already show. That paragraph can be shortened to one or
    two sentences that point to the figure and table rather than
    restating every number.

R5. Latency parity conclusion is stated in the abstract, Section III-A
    last paragraph, Section III-C first paragraph, and the conclusion.
    Three of the four occurrences are nearly verbatim. Consolidate to
    the Results section and a single reference in the conclusion.

R6. Safety-critical application requirements (collision avoidance,
    platooning, cooperative awareness) are listed in Background
    paragraph 4 and partially repeated in Section III-A last paragraph.
    The second mention adds nothing new and can be removed.

R7. Section III-A (Spectrum Resource Utilization Analysis) is largely
    qualitative restatement of the Method section mechanism, without
    introducing new quantitative results. Most of its content either
    repeats the SPS description (R2) or anticipates conclusions
    drawn in III-B. Consider collapsing III-A into the Method section
    or merging it with III-B.

PART 2: POSSIBLE REASONS FOR REJECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------

P1. Single scenario — insufficient generalization
    The entire quantitative evaluation rests on one traffic density
    (30 veh/km) and one propagation condition (LOS). Both the abstract
    and title use language implying a general comparative evaluation.
    Reviewers are likely to argue that a single operating point does
    not support broad conclusions about V2X feasibility, especially
    since PDR is known to be strongly density-dependent. The future
    work section acknowledges this, which may read as the authors
    admitting the paper is incomplete.

P2. No mitigation strategy evaluated
    Three mitigation strategies (digest-based certificates, hybrid
    schemes, infrastructure-assisted distribution) are proposed but
    none is evaluated. A reviewer expecting a complete contribution
    will note that the paper identifies a problem without offering
    a tested solution, limiting its actionable value.

P3. Digest-mode PDR is not reported
    The paper shows SPDU sizes for both cert and digest modes and
    notes that digest mode reduces overhead, but Figure 3 (PDR vs
    distance) shows only the default cert-mode PDR curves. A natural
    question the reviewer will ask is: does digest-mode operation
    bring Falcon-512 above the 90% threshold? Not answering this
    leaves a key result gap.

P4. No statistical rigor on PDR results
    PDR means are reported without confidence intervals, standard
    deviations, or significance tests. With 200 s of simulation data
    and a stochastic MAC scheduler, point estimates alone may not
    convince reviewers that the 23.92 pp gap is statistically robust
    rather than a simulation artifact.

P5. Simulator not validated against reference data
    The paper uses openCV2X, an academic simulator, without any
    validation against hardware measurements or 3GPP reference
    results. Reviewers familiar with C-V2X simulation will question
    whether the PDR and latency numbers are simulator-specific
    artifacts, particularly the counterintuitive result that
    Falcon-512 has marginally lower mean latency (51.82 ms) than
    ECDSA (51.92 ms). No explanation is offered for why the larger
    packet would produce lower latency.

P6. Claimed novelty is insufficiently differentiated from CHASM
    The paper positions itself against Twardokus and Rahbari (CHASM,
    2025), arguing that CHASM does not use a microscopic mobility
    simulator or quantify direct ECDSA-to-Falcon replacement. However,
    the distinction is made in a single sentence and the reviewer may
    find the delta too narrow to constitute an independent contribution,
    particularly since CHASM already validates a cross-layer approach
    with density variation.

P7. Title overstates scope
    "Comparative Performance Evaluation" implies a systematic
    comparison across multiple schemes, densities, or conditions.
    The paper evaluates one pair of algorithms under one scenario.
    A more accurate title would scope the contribution explicitly
    (e.g., "...under Moderate Urban Traffic Density").

P8. Section III-A adds no new data
    The Spectrum Resource Utilization subsection is entirely
    qualitative and restates mechanisms already explained in the
    Method. In a page-limited conference paper it occupies space
    that could hold the digest-mode PDR curve (P3) or a second
    density scenario (P1). Reviewers may flag it as padding.

P9. Falcon-512 lower latency is unexplained
    The abstract and Section III-C both report Falcon-512 mean
    latency (51.82 ms) as 0.10 ms lower than ECDSA (51.92 ms).
    For a larger packet that requires more subchannels, lower
    latency is counterintuitive. The paper does not explain this
    and a reviewer will likely ask for justification or flag it
    as a potential simulation artifact.

P10. Infrastructure-assisted distribution is undefined at first use
     in the conclusion (already flagged by reviewer; fix in progress).

P11. Methodology flowchart (Fig. 1) consumes a full column
     In a 6-page paper, a flowchart that largely restates the
     Method section text is a costly use of space. Reviewers
     may question whether the column would be better used for
     additional results.
