================================================================================
COHORT ASSIGNMENT LOGIC
================================================================================

K=2 (180° sectors):
  Cohort 0: 0° - 180°   (North → East → South approaches)
  Cohort 1: 180° - 360° (South → West → North approaches)

K=3 (120° sectors):
  Cohort 0: 0° - 120°   (North → East approaches)
  Cohort 1: 120° - 240° (Southeast → Southwest approaches)
  Cohort 2: 240° - 360° (West → Northwest approaches)

Formula: Cohort_ID = floor(heading_degrees / (360 / K))

================================================================================
RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

For V2X Safety Applications:

CHOOSE K=2 if:
  - Information freshness is critical (lower AoI priority)
  - Need better reliability (higher mean PDR)
  - Want lower interference levels
  - Trade-off: Accept 1% less CBR reduction

CHOOSE K=3 if:
  - Maximum CBR reduction is priority
  - Can tolerate higher AoI (still < 500ms threshold)
  - Trade-off: Accept more interference and older information

OPTIMAL CHOICE: K=2
  - Better balance between congestion control and safety
  - Lower AoI means fresher information for collision avoidance
  - Higher mean PDR means better overall reliability
  - Only 1% worse CBR reduction (still achieves 45% reduction!)
