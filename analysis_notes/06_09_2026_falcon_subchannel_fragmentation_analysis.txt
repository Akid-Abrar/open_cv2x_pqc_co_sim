# Technical Analysis Summary: PQC (Falcon-512) on C-V2X Mode 4 Sidelink
# Date: 06/09/2026

## 1. Protocol Stack Context

The layer immediately below the application layer is PDCP (Packet Data Convergence Protocol).
Below PDCP sits RLC, then MAC, and finally PHY. This layering is critical to understanding
the fragmentation behavior discovered throughout this analysis.

---

## 2. Subchannel Requirements for Falcon-512 Full Certificate at MCS 11

System config: 20 MHz, 100 RBs, 10 subchannels x 10 RBs, MCS range 5-11, adjacent mode
enabled (2 RBs deducted for SCI).

+-------------------------------+-----------+
| Component                     | Size      |
+-------------------------------+-----------+
| IEEE 1609.2 wrapper overhead  | 28 B      |
| J2735 BSMcoreData             | 43 B      |
| Certificate overhead          | 105 B     |
| Falcon-512 public key         | 897 B     |
| Falcon-512 signature (max)    | 666 B     |
| TOTAL                         | 1,739 B   |
|                               | 13,912 b  |
+-------------------------------+-----------+

MCS 11 -> 16QAM, iTBS 2 (offset: MCS 11 - 9 = 2). 7 subchannels required (68 effective
RBs -> TBS 14,112 bits), with only 200 bits of margin.

MCS 7 (QPSK) CANNOT carry the full cert even with all 10 subchannels (max TBS 12,576 bits).

---

## 3. Why subchannelsUsed Mean = 4 (Not 7)

Certificate interval (certInterval = 5): only every 5th BSM carries the full certificate.
The other 4 use an 8-byte digest.

- Digest SPDU: 745 B = 5,960 bits -> needs 4 subchannels at MCS 11 (TBS 7,736 bits)

RLC-layer fragmentation (UmTxEntity::rlcPduMake) is the key mechanism: when a packet
exceeds the grant's capacity, RLC sends what fits and holds the remainder in sduQueue_.
A full cert arriving on a 4-subchannel grant does NOT cause a grant break -- it is silently
fragmented.

Grant lifecycle:
  - First grant (bsmSeq=0, full cert) -> 7 subchannels
  - Grant expires (reselection counter 5-15, probResourceKeep = 0.4) -> 80% chance next
    packet is digest -> 4-subchannel grant
  - Once at 4 subchannels, cert arrivals don't break the grant (RLC fragments)
  - System settles into steady state of 4-subchannel grants

subchannelsUsed is a RECEIVER-SIDE PHY stat decoded from received SCIs
(LtePhyVUeMode4.cc:1512).

---

## 4. Why selectedMCS Averages ~6.5

MCS is recalculated EVERY transmission (LteMacVUeMode4.cc:1239-1262), not fixed per grant.
SAE J3161 skips MCS 8, 9, 10 -- only {5, 6, 7, 11} are available.

On a 4-subchannel grant (38 effective RBs):
  - Digest packets (5,960 bits) -> require MCS 11 (MCS 7 only gives 4,584 bits)
  - Cert fragments (smaller, variable) -> can use MCS 5, 6, or 7

The mix averages to ~6.5.

---

## 5. Subchannel vs MCS Behavior

- Subchannels: FIXED per grant -- only change on grant regeneration
- MCS: VARIABLE per transmission -- recalculated based on actual PDU size
- Grant breaks due to size mismatch never trigger because RLC always fragments to fit

---

## 6. Fragmentation Mechanics

- Fragmentation happens in the RLC layer (NOT PDCP), specifically UmTxEntity::rlcPduMake
  (src/stack/rlc/um/entity/UmTxEntity.cc:85-112)
- PHY sends each fragment in a separate subframe -- it has no knowledge of fragmentation
- Receiver's RLC reassembles via UmRxEntity::reassemble
- RLC UM has NO retransmission -- a lost fragment means the entire BSM is permanently lost

---

## 7. Cascading Fragmentation Effect

On a 4-subchannel grant at MCS 11, capacity is ~963 bytes per period. Full cert SPDU
(1,740 B with PDCP header) fragments into 2 pieces.

The RLC while loop packs multiple items from sduQueue_ (FIFO), so a remaining cert
fragment and part of the next BSM get packed into the same PDU. This creates cascading
fragmentation lasting ~4-5 periods (400-500ms). Since certInterval = 5 (cert every 500ms),
the queue barely recovers before the next cert arrives.

Detailed cascade trace on a 4-subchannel grant (capacity ~963 B per period):

  Period 0: Full cert SPDU arrives (1,740 B). First 963 B sent. 777 B remains.
  Period 1: New BSM (746 B) arrives. Queue: [777 B, 746 B].
            RLC sends: 777 B fragment + 186 B of BSM. 560 B BSM remains.
  Period 2: New BSM (746 B). Queue: [560 B, 746 B].
            RLC sends: 560 B + 403 B of BSM. 343 B remains.
  Period 3: New BSM (746 B). Queue: [343 B, 746 B].
            RLC sends: 343 B + 620 B of BSM. 126 B remains.
  Period 4: New BSM (746 B). Queue: [126 B, 746 B].
            RLC sends: 126 B + 746 B = 872 B. Queue empty. Back to normal.
  Period 5: Normal operation resumes (until next cert).

## 8. interPacketDelay Analysis

interPacketDelay measures time between consecutive successfully decoded TBs from the same
sender at the receiver PHY. Expected: 100ms. Observed: 150-180ms.

Using: Expected IPD = 100ms / (1 - loss_rate):

+---------------+--------------+
| Observed IPD  | TB Loss Rate |
+---------------+--------------+
| 150ms         | ~33%         |
| 180ms         | ~44%         |
+---------------+--------------+

---

## 9. Compounding Loss Effect (Critical Finding)

Because the full Falcon-512 cert is fragmented into 2 TBs, both must be received for
reassembly. The probability compounds:

+--------------+------------------+---------------+----------------+
| TB Loss Rate | P(cert received) | P(cert lost)  | P(digest lost) |
+--------------+------------------+---------------+----------------+
| 33%          | 0.67^2 = 45%     | 55%           | 33%            |
| 44%          | 0.56^2 = 31%     | 69%           | 44%            |
+--------------+------------------+---------------+----------------+

Full cert BSMs are roughly TWICE as likely to be lost compared to digest BSMs.

Implications:
  - The intended 1-in-5 certificate distribution is effectively much lower at the receiver
  - Receivers frequently lack valid certificates -> verification failures
  - This degradation is SPECIFIC TO PQC (Falcon-512) -- ECDSA certs fit in a single TB
    and do not suffer from this compounding loss
  - This represents a fundamental challenge for deploying post-quantum cryptography on
    C-V2X Mode 4 sidelink under current resource allocation parameters
